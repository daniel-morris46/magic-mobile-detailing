Wash and Polish

Remove swirls and enhance gloss

A detailed process to remove imperfections and enhance colour

Have you ever noticed swirl marks on your vehicle when it is sitting in the sun?

Swirl marks are micro scraches in your vehicle's paint caused by impropper car washing, unprotected paint, and everday use.

Polishing your vehicle removes these micro scratches by smoothing your vehicles clear coat giving it a clear, swirl free appearance.

We can not guarantee that every last scratch on your vehicles paint work will be removed as some deeper scratches can not be resolved with polishing.

Polishing is a detailed and labour intensive process. If you are looking for a simpler service to enhance gloss and shine on your vehicle check out our Wash and Wax service.

Our Wash and Polish Process

1

Give your vehicle our Premium Hand Wash.

2

Use an iron decontamination spray to remove brake dust and iron deposits from your vehicle's paintwork.

3

Clay bar the vehicles paint work to remove remaining imperfections and embedded road grit.

4

Towel dry with a soft microfiber drying towel.

5

Inspect your vehicle's paint and perform test spots as neccecary.

6

Machine polish with a variety of polishes and methods to ensure every painted surface is corrected.

7

Apply a high quality wax or sealant by hand or machine to the vehicle's paintwork.

8

Buff to a stunning shine.

Wash and Polish FAQs

Do you come to me?

Yes, we're a fully-contained mobile detailing unit. We can perform the wash at your home, apartment, or work location. We service Saskatoon, Warman, Martensville, Dundurn, and surrounding areas.

Why should I polish my vehicle?

You should have your vehicle polished if you would like to remove swirl marks from your vehicles paint, improve gloss, and make your vehicle easier to wash in the future.

Will polishing remove the scratches from my vehicle?

Polishing will only remove light scratches on your vehicle that have not gone too deep into your vehicles paintwork. Some scratches are too deep can only be repaired by repainting.
